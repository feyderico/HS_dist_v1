CORE

Idea

String theory assumes the existence of 10 dimensions: the familiar 4 extended dimensions plus a compact 6-dimensional space. The challenge to be solved is to find the right geometry of this 6D space that determines the physics of the universe in which we live. For this reason, the study of multi-dimensional spaces has grown considerably in the last 50 years. One of the simplest solutions is given by the 6d space called Calabi-Yau's Quintic which is our starting point. 

 This project is intended to be celebration of the imagination needed behinde each physics research. 

Implementation

Since it's impossible to visualize hyper-dimesional space, thus each space is a 2 dimesional projection. Hyper-spaces are algorothmically generated in every aspect .
Each space has its unique attributes set wich are determinated with a statistical pattern that gives them a scarcity rating.
Around the web there are many examples of Calabi-Yau visualization. One of the most relevant to mention is "A Construction for Computer Visualization of Certain Complex Curves" written by ******.

Attributes

There are 3 features that characterize each space

  • BASE NUMBERS: they define the Class of the space. There are 169 different classes.
  • TEXTURE: There are 72 different textures. 
  • RANDOMNESS: Many random numbers are used by the algorithm to generate the spaces. The two most relevant are the projection angle, wich influences the shape, and the distortion, wich influences the texture.
